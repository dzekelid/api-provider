description: The National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL), located in Golden, Colorado,
  is the United States primary laboratory for renewable energy and energy efficiency
  research and development. The National Renewable Energy Laboratory (NREL) is a government-owned,
  contractor-operated facility, and is funded through the U.S. Department of Energy
  (DOE). This arrangement allows a private entity to operate the lab on behalf of
  the federal government under a prime contract. NREL receives funding from Congress
  to be applied toward research and development projects. NREL also performs research
  on photovoltaics (PV) under the National Center for Photovoltaics. NREL has a number
  of PV research capabilities including research and development, testing, and deployment.
  NRELs campus houses several facilities dedicated to PV research.
